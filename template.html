Chunked Video Playback

The best thing since Sliced Bread

The Past

      // Simple HTTP video streaming
      netStream.play(url)
  • In the beginning Flash supported video playback and it was good. Video websites sprung up and multiplied. Whole loaves of Internet video were delivered to each and every home.

The New World

      // Simple chunked video streaming
      source = new HttpByteSource(url)
      netStream.appendBytes(source.read())
  • The Zeri project gave us a new API and we set out on our quest to build the most complicated video player ever
  • The new "appendBytes" API put us between the Internet and the plugin where we could deliver thick texas-toast sized slices of video to the plugin and build elaborate video sandwiches. This will be the meat of our talk.

But Why?

  • Efficiency
  • Dumber Servers, Smarter Clients
  • Support New Video Containers
  • Adaptive Bitrate Streaming

Efficiency

  • Chunked Byte Source

Dumber Servers, Smarter Clients

No longer a part of our servers

  • Server side throttling
  • "Pseudo-Streaming" via "start="


Now in our clients

  • Managed read-ahead
  • Client side seek indexing
  • turns out servers aren't the best at knowning what exactly got to the client, they tended to back off early and that caused rebuffers
  • describe pseudo streaming - eat just the middle of the loaf
  • servers had to handle both MP4 and FLV with start
